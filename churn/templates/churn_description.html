Unsatisfied clients might express their will to leave a specific company (BRAND). This is what we call churn. We are listing here a list of basic examples so you can understand it.

{#

Global roaming is now switched on

#}
No Source Text Label
1 BRAND-1 I am leaving <BRAND-1> at the end of the month! Churn
2 BRAND-1 I am fed up with <BRAND-1>! I’m switching to <BRAND-2>. Churn
3 BRAND-2 I am fed up with <BRAND-1>! I’m switching to <BRAND-2>. No churn
4 BRAND-1 I don't like <BRAND-1>! No churn
5 - I will leave your service at the end of the month! Churn


1. It is churn since the user is clear about his will to leave BRAND-1.

2-3. The text is the same for both examples, however the reference brand is different. In this case, BRAND-2 is not considered by the churny content since the user wants to join its service.

4. It is not churn as well since expressing disagreement with a service does not necessary mean you will quit it.

5. Here we don't have source brand. The user directly says he wants to leave the service.

With the bot, you can try yourself to enter text with either churny or non churny content. The aim of the bot will be to detect if the entry is churny or not. Please help us to improve the bot performance by giving it feedback on its answers.
